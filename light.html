Light Group

Overview

In response to water scarcity in Duhok, the Light Group from the University of Duhok explored wastewater treatment and reuse in car wash services through a Participatory Action Research (PAR) project.

What We Did

  • Assessed public opinion and technical feasibility of eco-friendly car wash systems
  • Explored treatment methods: settling tanks, hydro cyclone systems, UV disinfection
  • Collected data via:
    • 26 interviews
    • 3 focus group discussions
    • 91 surveys

Key Findings

  • 41.7% use car washes weekly, mostly in summer
  • 86% support eco-friendly car wash systems
  • 66% are willing to pay more for recycled water use
  • Main barriers:
    • 56% lack of government support
    • 48% low awareness
    • 44% high costs
  • Community Feedback:
    • Strong support from public and stakeholders
    • Demand for government funding, training, and awareness campaigns

Team Recommendations

  • Government funding and regulation for eco-car washes
  • UNICEF-led awareness campaigns
  • Technical workshops for operators
  • Incentives for water recycling systems
  • Limit home car washing to encourage sustainable practices

Conclusion

Wastewater reuse in car wash services offers a realistic and supported solution to Duhokâ€™s water crisis. With public backing, education, and regulation, this initiative can lead the way in sustainable urban water management.
